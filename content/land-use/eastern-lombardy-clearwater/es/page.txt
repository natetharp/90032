<a name="background"></a>
<h2>Background</h2>

<p>In an October 2013 ruling, the Los Angeles Planning Commission denied plans to develop a 20 unit apartment complex, a 45,000 sq. ft. school facility and a 2,300 sq. ft. commercial building. The Commission called the plans "struggling, "disturbing", and stated they "hope the revised project will be more thoughtful." A sale of the land is now pending. Preliminary plans are to construct 43 new single-family homes across the hillside.</p>

<p>El Sereno, with its open hillsides, small town character, and lack of a community plan to guide responsible growth, repeatedly falls prey to ambitious developers. Housing Developments, or 'Master Planned Communities' as they're also known, can be great money makers for developers but are historically notorious for introducing a culture of divisiveness, especially in small but well-populated neighborhoods like El Sereno.</p>

<p>On the parcel at Eastern and Lombardy, a housing development would stand in stark contrast to the surrounding residential properties which have developed over time to form a culturally and economically diverse community.</p>

<blockquote>
<p>"These places [housing developments] are built usually without regard to the local culture, environment or climate, so it's always the same kind of architecture you stamp on every location."</p>
<cite><a href="http://www.wired.com/2014/05/robert-harding-pittman-anonymization/">Robert Harding Pittman</a>, Environmental Engineer, author of <em><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Anonymization-Global-Proliferation-Urban-Sprawl/dp/3868282882">Anonymization</a></em></cite>
</blockquote>

<p>El Sereno is a well-populated urban neighborhood. Classic, successful planning for urban areas incorporates multiple small playgrounds, plazas, and parks within walking distance of residential areas, providing for both active and passive use by all ages.</p>

<p>Over 20% of El Sereno's population is age 50 or older and nearly 10% is over age 65. Studies are now beginning to shed light on barriers to park use faced by seniors. Additionally, The Trust for Public Land reports that "El Sereno... has one of the greatest concentrations of children under five in [Los Angeles] county—but not nearly enough parks or playgrounds to serve them."</p>

<p>In a recent panel discussion on park accessibility hosted by the California State Parks Commission, Barbara Romero, former Chief of Urban Projects at MRCA stated the "best way to improve access is to build parks where the people are."</p>

<p>The parcel at Eastern/Lombardy is well situated to serve as a neighborhood park offering not only a playground and fitness circuit, but also a community garden, hillside nature trails or other passive-use features that would benefit all members of the local community. It would fulfill different needs than those served by the adjacent sports and recreation center.</p>

<p>The addition of nearly 50 new homes to this single parcel amounts to creating a segmented residential area within our existing residential area, one that "dominates rather than accommodates nature," as Wired magazine, has described housing developments.</p>

<p>Instead, this community can work to create a lasting asset of this location, preserve and provide access to our natural history, and provide for our social, physical, and environmental well-being.</p>

<h4>What's so bad about single-family homes?</h4>

<p>Some people in the community have said three or four single family homes might be ok for the property and could fit with the residential neighborhood. Unfortunately, in order to pay for the property and meet their financial goals, any developer will need to build many many more houses on the site.  Three or four homes would not be financially feasible and so is not a realistic possibility for the property.</p>

<h4>Won't new housing increase property values in the neighborhood?</h4>

<p>Builders often say this to win support from neighboring residents. Actually, it is neighborhood and property improvements and community conveniences make a neighborhood more desirable, therefore increasing property values.  An example is the recent trend of contractors purchasing foreclosed homes for renovation and resale to new buyers. As a result, prices have risen in El Sereno, Highland Park and Boyle Heights.</p>

<p>These block by block improvements and gradual arrival of new neighbors is a demonstration of healthy development within the existing structure of our community.</p>


<h4>Who is the developer buying the property?</h4>

<p>A sale is pending to <a href="http://www.clearwatercommunities.com/contact/">Clearwater Communities</a>, a large development company based in Newport Beach.</p>

<p>Developments of this type are a sizeable investment, even for a company like Clearwater. Before the sale is finalized, they meet with city planners, DOT, CD14 staff, and even Councilman Huizar to see whether they will encounter any barriers to their plans. Land development is a business and nothing more. If the developer finds he is unable to build as many homes as needed to turn a sizeable profit, they will walk away from the sale.</p>


<h4>Why a park across the street from a park?</h4>

<p>The El Sereno recreation center, its swimming pool, playing field, skate park and sports courts is used heavily by the community for many types of active athletic activities and even private events.  
In urban areas everywhere, successful neighborhoods include many small playgrounds, plazas, and parks within walking distance of residential areas to provide for both 'active' and 'passive' use by all people, from the youngest to the oldest.</p>

<p>Over 20% of El Sereno's population is age 50 or older and nearly 10% is over age 65. The Trust for Public Land reports that "El Sereno... has one of the greatest concentrations of children under five in [Los Angeles] county—but not nearly enough parks or playgrounds to serve them."</p>

<p>In a recent panel discussion on park accessibility hosted by the California State Parks Commission, Barbara Romero, former Chief of Urban Projects at MRCA stated the "best way to improve access is to build parks where the people are."</p>

<p>The land at Eastern/Lombardy is well situated to serve as a 'passive use' neighborhood park offering not only a playground and fitness circuit, but also a community garden, hillside nature trails or other non-athletic features that would benefit all members of the local community. It would fulfill different needs than those served by the adjacent sports and recreation center.</p>

<h4>What if I'd like to see a dog park, mountain bike trails or something else on the site?</h4>
 
<p>The first step is to secure the land for public use and stop the yearly battles with developers.  Planning for public space will include lots of opportunities for public comment and what develops will be a place that belongs to everyone. That means we all have a responsibility to use and maintain it, as well as guard it against misuse.  In the meanwhile, there is an <a href="/eastern-lombardy-survey/">online survey</a> you and others can take to share ideas about park space.</p>

<h2>preguntas frecuentes</h2>
 
¿Qué hay de malo en viviendas unifamiliares?
Algunas personas de la comunidad han dicho tres o cuatro viviendas unifamiliares pueden estar bien para la propiedad y podría caber con el barrio residencial. Desafortunadamente, con el fin de pagar por la propiedad y cumplir con sus objetivos financieros, cualquier desarrollador tendrá que construir muchas, muchas más casas en el sitio. Tres o cuatro casas no serían financieramente factible y lo que no es una posibilidad realista para la propiedad.
Así que, ¿qué pasa con un gran desarrollo de la vivienda?
 
Urbanizaciones, o 'Maestro Comunidades Planificadas', ya que también son conocidos, pueden ser grandes fabricantes de dinero para los desarrolladores, pero son históricamente conocidos por la introducción de una cultura de la división, especialmente en las pequeñas pero bien pobladas barrios como El Sereno. La construcción de cerca de 50 nuevas viviendas en esta sola parcela reduce a crear una población segmentada dentro de nuestra comunidad existente.
En el sustantivo en Eastern y Lombardy, una promoción de viviendas se situaría en marcado contraste con las propiedades residenciales de los alrededores que se han desarrollado a través del tiempo para formar una comunidad cultural y económicamente diversa.
 
"Estos lugares [urbanizaciones] se construyen por lo general sin tener en cuenta la cultura local, el medio ambiente o el clima, por lo que es siempre el mismo tipo de arquitectura que se estampe en cada ubicación."
— Robert Harding Pittman, Ingeniero Ambiental, Autor de "Anonymization"
 
No lo haré nuevos valores de la propiedad aumentan la vivienda en el barrio?
Constructores suelen decir esto para ganar el apoyo de los residentes vecinos. En realidad, es barrio y mejoras a la propiedad y el confort de la comunidad a hacer un barrio más deseable, por lo tanto, el aumento de valor de las propiedades. Un ejemplo es la tendencia reciente de los contratistas de compras de casas embargadas en renovación y reventa a nuevos compradores. Como resultado, los precios han subido en El Sereno, Highland Park y Boyle Heights.
Estas mejoras bloque por bloque y la llegada progresiva de nuevos vecinos es una demostración de un desarrollo saludable dentro de la estructura existente de nuestra comunidad.

¿Por qué un parque al otro lado de la calle de un parque?
El centro de El Sereno recreación, la piscina, el campo de juego, pista de patinaje y canchas deportivas se utiliza en gran medida por la comunidad para muchos tipos de actividades deportivas activas e incluso eventos privados.

En las zonas urbanas de todo el mundo, los barrios exitosos incluyen numerosas zonas de juego pequeños, plazas y parques, a poca distancia de las zonas residenciales para ofrecer, tanto para uso "activo" "pasiva" por todas las personas, desde el más joven hasta el más viejo.
 
Más del 20% de la población de El Sereno es mayor de 50 años y casi el 10% tiene más de 65 años de edad. The Trust for Public Land informa que "El Sereno ... tiene una de las mayores concentraciones de niños menores de cinco años en [Los Angeles] provincia -pero no lo suficiente como parques o zonas de juegos para servirles. "
 
En una reciente mesa redonda sobre la accesibilidad del parque organizada por la Comisión de Parques del Estado de California, Barbara Romero, ex Jefe de Proyectos Urbanos de MRCA declaró "la mejor manera de mejorar el acceso es la construcción de parques donde la gente es."
 
La tierra en la costa este / Lombardía está bien situado para servir como un 'uso pasivo "parque del barrio ofreciendo no sólo un parque infantil y un circuito de fitness, sino también un jardín comunitario, senderos naturales de las laderas o de otras características no atléticas que beneficiaría a todos los miembros de la comunidad local. Sería cumplir con las diferentes necesidades que los atendidos por el centro deportivo y de ocio adyacente.
 
¿Qué pasa si me gustaría ver a un parque para perros, en bicicleta de montaña o cualquier otra cosa en el sitio?
El primer paso es asegurar la tierra para el uso público y detener los combates anuales con los desarrolladores. La planificación de los espacios públicos incluirá un montón de oportunidades para comentarios del público y lo que se desarrolla será un lugar que es de todos. Eso significa que todos tenemos la responsabilidad de usar y mantener, así como proteger contra el uso indebido. En el mientras tanto, hay una encuesta en línea que usted y otros puedan tener para compartir ideas sobre el espacio parque.
